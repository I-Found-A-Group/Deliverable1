Benfords Law, or the first-digit law, refers to the fact that many sets of real-life numerical data probabilistically conform to certain patterns. Essentially, it observes that the leading digit of entries is disproportionately likely to be small, with 1 appearing as the first digit roughly 30% of the time. This law applies over a wide variety of data sets, including building heights, street addresses, prices, and population numbers. It has been used to identify fraud, as artificially created data - say, for fake customer lists, or for falsifying elections - will often not conform to Benfords Law.
